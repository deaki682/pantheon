## The pattern the sweep revealed

The long-factor space is **spanned** — net-issuance, gross-profitability, and
cash-based profitability are all the same two axes (capital-return + quality),
and every new *factor* proposed collapsed into them or into the graveyard. The
live edge has moved to **structure and text**: forced sellers, contractual
events, and document-reads. Every 4/5+ candidate is either an event with a
forced counterparty, an avoidance overlay, or a tail-preserving combination —
not a new long factor. That convergence IS the finding.

## Kills / traps (do NOT spend a slug)

- **Sin/exclusion premium** — TRAP: relabel of owned profitability + investment (Blitz-Fabozzi 2017).
- **Earnings persistence / smoothness / low earnings-vol** — Frankel-Litov directly tested and rejected the return claim; accruals/low-vol relabel.
- **NOA / ΔNWC / inventory-DSO / capex-intensity** — dead relabels of accruals + asset-growth (their own papers define NOA as cumulative accruals).
- **Piotroski F / Mohanram G (naive)** — ~5/9 signals are owned profitability + net-issuance; only an orthogonalized residual is worth a test.
- **Seasonality** (Halloween, pre-FOMC [disappeared post-2015], turn-of-month, Dec/Jan) — G1-fail; only October-RIC (2.5/5) has a real forcing and it fails G3/G5.
- **ROIC-WACC spread** — the WACC leg smuggles dead asset-growth + low-vol back in.
- **Forced flows / ESG-downgrade** — dead by the same Greenwood-Sammon 2025 literature as the house's index-effect graveyard.
