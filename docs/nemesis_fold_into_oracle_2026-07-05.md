# Nemesis folded into Oracle (2026-07-05, operator directive)

## The decision

Nemesis is **retired as a standalone god.** Spinoff forced-selling becomes a
**sourcing channel inside reframed-Oracle** (which already hunts forced sellers);
the `nemesis/` package — especially the Form 10 reading — is **kept as a library**
Oracle imports. Nemesis's ~$2,000 sleeve winds to cash and returns to the
treasury.

## Why (the same pattern, cleaner than Achilles)

1. **The mechanical premise has no measured alpha.** Buy-all spinoffs returned
   +41% vs SPY +30% — but **trailed IWM (small-caps) at +45%**. Against the
   correct benchmark spinoffs *underperformed*; the +41% was small-cap beta. The
   follow-up confirmed it: −1.0%/event across normal/bear vintages (t −0.2) — the
   warm-vintage +41% did not generalize.
2. **The claimed real edge — the veto (avoiding bad spinoffs) — is unproven.** It
   "worked" on 3 pre-registered names (it dodged a −58% blowup). n=3 is a story,
   not evidence.
3. **A live sleeve years from a verdict.** Nemesis's own exit-curve study says
   spinoffs need a 7–9 month hold; the checkpoint is ≥20 graded trades and it had
   **0 graded** (just went live days ago). That's ~2–3 years to a verdict on real
   money — the slowest in the house, with no evidence yet.
4. **Its first live pick failed the floor test.** The one live order, **OCTV**,
   is a name the 2026-07-05 forced-seller scan independently rejected as
   NOT-convex (large, levered software carve-out, no asset floor). Oracle's convex
   discipline (floor + catalyst + structural mispricing) *refuses* it. The order
   was queued (unfilled) over the July 4th weekend and was **cancelled** before
   Monday's open as part of this fold; the sleeve entry was reversed.

## Why fold into Oracle specifically (not just retire)

Reframed-Oracle already hunts **forced sellers**, and **spinoffs are one of its
channels** — RNA (a spinoff) is in Oracle's convex book *right now*, and it
*passed* the floor test where OCTV *failed*. Nemesis is a specialized case of
what Oracle now does, and Oracle's machinery is demonstrably better at Nemesis's
own job (it rejects the floorless names Nemesis bought). Folding consolidates the
entire forced-seller thesis into ONE measured engine (with the A/B) instead of
two overlapping live experiments — and keeps the genuinely useful spinoff/Form 10
sourcing as an Oracle channel.

## What changes

- **Nemesis the god is retired.** `/nemesis` → wind-down / library-reference
  only; `NEMESIS_LIVE=false`. Sleeve wound to cash (~$2,000), **returned to the
  treasury** for the pending allocation. Guard file kept.
- **The `nemesis/` package is KEPT as a library** (the Buzz/Catalyst/Achilles
  precedent). Oracle imports it for spinoff sourcing + Form 10 reading; any
  spinoff candidate must pass the **convex-dossier discipline** (floor + catalyst
  + structural mispricing + the already-fired guard) like every other Oracle name
  — so a floorless levered carve-out (OCTV) never enters.
- **Oracle gains an explicit spinoff channel.** Spinoff-orphan forced-selling
  joins index-deletion / sub-NAV / distressed as an idea-source in the Oracle
  lenses step; the veto (avoid bad spinoffs) is subsumed by the convex writer's
  refusal to build a dossier without a floor.
- `/nemesis-ghost` (if useful) may keep shadowing on paper for the record.

## What does NOT change

- No spinoff backtest may be cited as evidence FOR the strategy (the Delphi rule);
  the forced-seller edge is measured only via Oracle's live A/B (dossier vs screen).
- Oracle's own discipline is unchanged — spinoffs are just another channel feeding
  the same convex writer and the same A/B.
