## The mission (honest floor-plus-option framing)

Pantheon is a **fully managed, fully agentic portfolio whose purpose is to grow
funds.** Growth rests on two things that must not be confused:

- **Beta (Plutus) is the reliable FLOOR** — the foundation, not the headline. It
  is long large-cap equity and carries the market's own compounding (~7–10%/yr
  over time). It is where the dollar growth actually comes from. Its net-issuance
  factor edge is thin (it ~ties SPY equal-weight); the *beta* is the point.
- **The experiments (Hermes / Oracle / Proteus + Plutus's LLM overlay) are the
  right-tail OPTION** — cheap, measured, and **unproven.** Beta is capped at
  market returns by construction, so the experiments are the *only* path to
  *outsized* growth. They are sized at ~58% as a **conscious conviction bet that
  a real LLM edge exists** — NOT as a claim that it has been proven.

**Growth = the beta floor + the option value** of an experiment proving a real,
forward-confirmed LLM-lift and getting concentrated behind. That is the whole
machine.

## What the day's research established (the honest priors this mandate rests on)

- **There is no scalable alpha edge for this book** (growth-hunt: `LEAN_ON_BETA`,
  zero scalable-engine survivors). Every mechanical buy-signal the house tested
  is refuted; the one supported factor (net-issuance) ties SPY.
- **The LLM's only measured-real contribution so far is AVOIDANCE and
  anti-overtrading discipline** — not a scalable buy-signal.
- **The durable high-excess edges (forced-seller / event-driven) are
  capacity-capped diversifiers**, not engines — and two (post-BK, spinoff-orphans)
  already failed their tests this session.
- **Therefore we are betting ~58% of the book on an edge we have NOT yet proven
  exists.** This mandate states that plainly so no future session mistakes the
  bet for a fact. The bet is defensible — it's the only shot at the right tail —
  but it is a bet, priced honestly, not a certainty.

## The scorecard

Judged on BOTH:
1. **Growth** — does the whole book compound at a good clip? (The floor does most
   of this early.)
2. **LLM-lift** — do the A/Bs prove the LLM beats a screen? This is what justifies
   *concentrating capital behind a winner* — the only way the option pays off. If
   after a real sample NO experiment shows a forward-real lift, the honest move is
   to shrink the experiments toward the beta floor. The bet is not permanent; the
   A/Bs adjudicate it.

NOT scored on beating SPY's raw bull return, NOT on Sharpe in a vacuum. The bar is
**real growth from the beta floor, plus an honestly-measured verdict on whether
the LLM option is worth its 58%.** Retire what fails, concentrate behind what
proves out.

- Not "experiments as the growth engine" (the refuted framing — beta is the engine).

- Not a hedge fund optimizing Sharpe for its own sake.
- Not a claim that the LLM edge is proven — it is a **measured, priced bet** that
  it exists, adjudicated by the A/Bs and the LLM-instrument track (below).
