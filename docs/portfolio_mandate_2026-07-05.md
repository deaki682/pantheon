# Pantheon portfolio mandate (2026-07-05, operator directive)

## The mission

Pantheon is a **fully managed, fully agentic portfolio whose purpose is to grow
funds — headlined by the LLM-edge experiments.** Not a research sandbox that
happens to hold money, and not a passive index tracker. Every dollar is worked by
a god; the differentiator — the *story* — is whether an LLM's judgment can find
edges a screen can't (the A/Bs). Growth is the goal; the experiments are the
engine of growth.

## The three implications (how this shapes the book)

1. **Grow funds → participate, don't sit out.** Absolute return matters, so the
   book must not be structurally short equity beta. The participation comes from
   **Plutus** (large-cap net-issuance) — an *agentic* growth anchor that carries
   market beta AND a proven factor edge — not from a passive SPY core.
2. **Headlined by experiments → the LLM-edge gods lead.** Hermes, Oracle, and
   Proteus get the prominent, collective majority of capital and are the marquee.
   They sit ON TOP of the Plutus anchor and aim to beat it. Their A/B verdicts
   (LLM-lift) are the headline result the whole book is built to produce.
3. **Fully managed → no dead cash.** Idle cash is the worst of all worlds for a
   growth mandate (no beta, no alpha, no floor). Cash is an operational buffer
   only; capital is deployed into the bounded-floor gods, which give downside
   protection AND upside.

## The scorecard

Judged on BOTH, not either/or:
1. **Growth** — does the whole book compound at a good clip (the mandate)?
2. **The experiments' edge** — do the A/Bs (Hermes LLM-lift first, then Oracle,
   Proteus's graded book) prove the LLM beats a screen? A validated edge is what
   justifies *scaling the winner* — the engine of future growth.

NOT scored on beating SPY's raw return in a bull (we don't chase refuted
momentum) and NOT on risk-adjusted alpha in a vacuum. The bar is **real growth,
with the experiments as the mechanism that makes growth compound faster over
time** — retire what fails, scale what proves out (the self-improving allocation).

## What this is NOT
- Not a passive core-satellite with an index core — the "core" is agentic (Plutus).
- Not a hedge fund optimizing Sharpe for its own sake — the goal is growth.
- Not a pure knowledge experiment — the money is meant to grow, measured in dollars.
