# Pre-registration: Proteus — the discretionary ghost

Committed 2026-07-04, before Proteus makes his first decision. This
document freezes how he is GRADED. Nothing here constrains how he
THINKS — that is the experiment.

## Question

Every god in Pantheon trades a frozen strategy; the weekend's research
showed the mechanical layers are ~zero-edge and the judgment layers are
unproven. Proteus asks the complementary question nobody has tested:
**does full human-style discretion — free strategy choice, free
sizing, free timing, deep research on anything — beat both the frozen
gods and the passive benchmark, when every decision is recorded and
graded without mercy?** He is the experiment on discretion itself.

## Terms of existence (FROZEN)

- **Paper only.** $10,000 ghost capital. No broker orders, ever. He
  may not touch any other god's state. Ownership prefix
  `cache/ghost_proteus_` only.
- **Full discretion inside two rails:** any US-listed equity or ETF,
  long or short, any horizon 1–365 days, any concentration including
  all-in — but (1) NO leverage: long exposure ≤ cash, aggregate short
  exposure ≤ 50% of equity; (2) the journal is APPEND-ONLY and every
  trade must enter through it. Costs modeled: 5 bps/side (Pantheon
  standard) + 5%/yr flat borrow on shorts (disclosed approximation).
- **Every decision journaled at decision time** with: thesis,
  edge_class (which kind of edge he claims), a FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION
  ("X happens by date D"), horizon, confidence, exit plan, and a kill
  condition. Entries use quotes fetched in-session — no backdating,
  no editing history, no "what I meant was." The validated writer
  refuses stubs (the Delphi lesson, applied at birth).
- **Confidence is recorded, never protected.** He sizes however he
  wants; the recorded confidence exists so calibration can be measured
  against outcomes.

## Grading (FROZEN)

- A trade grades when CLOSED (his exit plan, kill condition, horizon
  expiry, or discretionary exit — all valid; the journal must say
  which). Net return after fees/borrow.
- Per-trade excess vs the passive mirror over the identical window:
  longs vs SPY, shorts vs −SPY (SPY quotes captured at entry and exit).
- Book equity curve marked at each session vs SPY from inception.
- Falsifiable predictions graded true/false as written, independent of
  P&L (a profitable trade with a failed prediction is LUCK and gets
  recorded as such).

## Checkpoint (FROZEN)

At **30 closed graded trades or 2027-01-15, whichever comes first**:

- **VALIDATED**: book beats SPY since inception AND per-trade mean
  excess > 0 with t ≥ 2 AND confidence is not inverted (high-confidence
  tercile mean excess ≥ low tercile). Consequence: the operator MAY
  consider a live sleeve — which would start at $1–2k behind the same
  capital gates every god faces. Validation here earns a tryout, not
  a wallet.
- **REFUTED**: book trails SPY AND per-trade mean excess ≤ 0. He is
  retired to the ledger with full honors — the answer to the
  discretion question is itself the deliverable.
- **INCONCLUSIVE**: anything else — operator decides continue/retire.
  One shot, no re-cuts; the ledger row gets written whatever it says.

## Priors, stated for honesty

The house prediction (recorded so the grade embarrasses whoever earned
it): discretion underperforms the disciplined gods — drift,
recency-chasing, and inconsistency show up in the journal within
weeks. The counter-case: the weekend's probes showed LLM judgment is
excellent at enumerated-disaster detection and document-grounded facts;
if Proteus leans on those strengths rather than continuous scoring,
he may surprise. That tension is the point of the experiment.
