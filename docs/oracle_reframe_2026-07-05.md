# Oracle reframe — from insider-signal factor god to measured deep-research convex engine (2026-07-05)

## The diagnosis (why the reframe)

Oracle was built pre-gauntlet as a **mechanical signal** strategy: four lenses
(insider clusters, 13F smart money, activist 13D, quality prescreen) score
names → `size_book` ranks by conviction → an 8-name equal-ish cohort held 12
months. Everything the house has since measured says that spine is wrong:

- **The insider-cluster signal was refuted** — the complete-population replay
  read −6.4%/yr vs IWM (n=291, win 32%); the **quality lens was the drag**;
  only 13D showed a positive sign (docs/oracle_replay_results_2026-07.md,
  RESEARCH_LEDGER 2026-07-03).
- The gauntlet's whole lesson: **mechanical buy-triggers measure ~zero**;
  "insiders bought / high quality / trades cheap" are TABLE STAKES, never a
  thesis (RESEARCH_LEDGER; the Proteus priors).
- The 8-name equal cohort is **anti-convex by construction** — diversified into
  a benchmark-hugger, the convexity of any real winner averaged away
  (docs/return_convexity_pivot_2026-07-04.md).

So if Oracle has ANY edge, it is not in the lenses — it is in the **dossiers**:
the deep LLM fundamental research on each neglected name. Which means Oracle is
really the **same shape as Hermes and Lens-B** — a mechanical filter narrows the
universe, and an LLM deep-read is the actual decision — but it has been running
as a *factor god* on a *refuted signal*. The reframe recasts it as what it
actually is.

## The reframe (three moves)

1. **Lenses → idea-sourcing only.** The four lenses stop being "the signal" and
   become a cheap net to surface *candidates worth researching*. No mechanical
   selection or conviction-ranking on refuted signals.
2. **Dossier → the measured edge.** The deep read becomes the decision, written
   for ASYMMETRY (below) and graded, so we find out — not assume — whether the
   research is real.
3. **Concentrate.** Drop the 8-name equal cohort. Express real conviction with
   **few, weighted positions** (concentration is where a small book's return
   lives). Patience and deep research are the edge, not diversification.

## How dossiers are written now (the new spec — the heart of the reframe)

The OLD dossier confirmed the signals: "strong insider buying, high quality,
trades cheap." Every one of those triggers is dead. The NEW dossier's job is to
**establish asymmetry and name the structural reason the price is wrong.** Every
dossier must answer, in writing:

1. **The floor (bounded downside).** What caps the loss? Asset/liquidation value,
   net cash, a hard book floor, a take-private floor. Quantify it:
   `floor_pct` = the worst plausible drawdown if the thesis is wrong. A dossier
   with no identifiable floor is a growth gamble, not an Oracle name.
2. **The upside (magnitude + path).** Not "it's undervalued" — the SPECIFIC path
   to a large re-rating and its magnitude (`upside_x`). What has to happen, and
   what is it worth if it does.
3. **Why it's mispriced — STRUCTURALLY (the G2 gate).** Name the reason a real
   edge exists: **neglect** (no/thin analyst coverage, too small for funds),
   **a forced seller** (index deletion, tax-loss, spinoff-orphan, distressed
   holder), or **a hard catalyst** the market hasn't priced. "The market
   underappreciates this quality company" is a drift-forecast — refuted. If the
   dossier can't name the structural mispricing, it does not enter the book.
4. **The catalyst (contractual > forecast — the G4 gate).** Anchor to a HARD
   event where possible (asset sale, pending transaction, spinoff, tender,
   refinancing, a dated decision) — worth far more than "earnings should
   improve someday." Record `catalyst` + `catalyst_date`.
5. **A falsifiable prediction + typed kill condition** (the Proteus discipline):
   a dated, checkable claim, and a `kill_condition` with a type
   (`price_level` | `drawdown_pct` | `thesis_date` | `filing_event`) so the
   fresh instance can look up "did it fire," not re-litigate prose.
6. **The adversarial pass.** One honest paragraph: "what does the disciplined
   house know that says this is a mistake?" If the thesis reduces to a refuted
   trigger (insider/quality/cheap) or a base-rate violation with no stated
   reason, it is cut BEFORE the book, not defended after.

The dossier is scored on **asymmetry** (`upside_x` × probability vs `floor_pct`),
not on a lens-conviction number. Conviction, if used, sizes within the book —
it is not the selection signal.

## The measurement (don't assume the edge — prove it)

"The edge is in the dossiers" is a HYPOTHESIS, and exactly the comforting story
the house has been burned by. So the reframe measures it, Hermes-style:

- **Oracle A/B (`oracle.ab`):** the LLM's dossier-selected book (concentrated,
  conviction-weighted) vs a **mechanical baseline** — the top-N by lens score
  from the SAME candidate pool, equal-weight. Both graded on realized excess.
- **Oracle LLM-lift = dossier book − screen baseline.** Positive and material →
  the dossiers add real alpha; Oracle earns concentration and capital. Zero or
  negative → the dossiers are elaborate rationalization and Oracle folds into
  Proteus. The numbers decide.

## Honest caveats

- **Overlap with Proteus.** A reframed Oracle (LLM fundamental research on
  mispriced small/mid-caps) resembles a specialized Proteus. The distinction
  that justifies keeping it separate: Oracle is **patient and deep** (multi-
  month horizons, complete dossiers on neglected names, a maintained research
  pool), where Proteus is **opportunistic and broad**. Valid only if the dossier
  edge measures real — else Oracle should merge into Proteus.
- **Unproven.** No validated live track record; the reframe is a better-
  disciplined bet than the refuted signal it replaces, not a proven winner.
- **Small capital.** Modest dollar stakes; the product is the measured answer to
  "does the deep research beat the screen," which is the same question Hermes
  asks — Oracle asks it on the *value/neglect* side, Hermes on the *event* side.
