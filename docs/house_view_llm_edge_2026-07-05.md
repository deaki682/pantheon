# House view — where the LLM edge actually is, and how to stay ahead of decay (2026-07-05)

A strategic prior for the lab and every god session. Not a strategy — a *map of
where to look and where not to*, from the external evidence + this house's own
findings. Read it before proposing a new hypothesis or reaching for a "new" edge.

## What the evidence says (LLMs DO have a market edge — a specific one)

The academic record (2023–2026) is real but narrow:
- **The edge is in READING unstructured text into a signal.** Lopez-Lira & Tang
  (2023): GPT sentiment on news headlines predicts out-of-sample returns
  (~90% initial-reaction hit rate). Financial reasoning *emerges* with model size.
- **It's strongest in SMALL stocks and after NEGATIVE news** — the neglected,
  hard-to-arbitrage corners. (Same paper.)
- **It DECAYS as adoption rises.** Lopez-Lira: strategy returns fall as LLM use
  grows; the market gets more efficient. By 2026 headline sentiment is spent.
- **Look-ahead / training-leakage is a real but *modest* threat for short-horizon
  return prediction** — chronologically-consistent models (ChronoBERT) perform
  about as well, so the reading edge is partly *genuine*, not pure memorization.
- **Transaction costs + output variability kill a lot of it.** After costs,
  "thinking" LLMs show no net advantage; variability drives overtrading.
- **The one-line summary of every real-world test: LLMs are good at *research*,
  bad at *execution*** (timing, sizing, overtrading). Good at reading; bad at trading.

## What that means for THIS house (mostly: we're already aligned)

Our design is, almost point-for-point, the correct response to the evidence —
keep it:
- LLM does **judgment on documents** (Oracle dossiers, Hermes deal-break reads),
  never price-timing → harvest the reading edge, avoid the execution weakness.
- We hunt **small/micro-caps + forced-sellers/distressed** → exactly the
  neglected/negative corner where the edge is strongest and least arbitraged.
- We are **deliberately low-turnover** → dodge the variability→overtrading→cost
  death spiral that's the #1 killer in the literature.
- Execution (sizing, stops, rebalancing) is **mechanical rules**, not LLM → guard
  the exact thing LLMs do badly.
- Every LLM edge is **A/B-measured** (LLM vs screen) → we find out if it's real
  instead of assuming, and we *see the decay* when the crowd arrives.

## The decay reality → you must ROTATE, not settle

Every specific edge crowds and fades (McLean-Pontiff ~58% factor decay;
Lopez-Lira's LLM decay). So no single strategy is the asset. **The asset is the
machine that finds edges and retires them faster than they crowd** — the lab, the
gauntlet, the A/Bs, the willingness to fold a god (Delphi/Midas/Achilles/Nemesis).
Treat the A/B *lift shrinking* as the crowd arriving: rotate before it hits zero.

## How to look ahead: MAP EVERY EDGE BY ITS BARRIER

An edge exists because a barrier stops others from taking it; it decays when the
barrier falls. Before proposing a hypothesis, name its barrier and ask *does LLM
adoption dissolve it?*

**AVOID — barriers LLMs are dissolving now (decaying edges):**
- "Processing this text is hard" → news sentiment (dead), headline 10-K reads (crowding).
- "Research effort per dollar is high" → falling fast wherever an LLM can do the work.

**CAMP ON — barriers LLMs CANNOT dissolve (durable edges):**
- **Structural constraint** — a forced seller (index deletion, bankruptcy, mandate,
  redemption). Adoption can't un-force a forced counterparty. *Most durable.* This
  is Oracle's whole thesis and where the house core should live.
- **Capacity** — too small for big money (micro/nano). Durable by arithmetic.
- **Patience** — multi-year horizons most capital can't hold.
- **Execution** — LLMs are bad here, so edges guarded by it don't crowd.

**RIDE (briefly) — the capability frontier (window edges):**
Each new model capability makes a new data type processable-into-signal, with a
lag before the deployed fleet exploits it. Be early, keep moving. Current windows
the fleet mostly ISN'T exploiting:
- **Long-context *full-document* synthesis** (the entire docket + all exhibits +
  litigation history, not the headline).
- **Agentic cross-referencing** (connect an index-deletion filing + a distressed
  holder + a price dislocation into one thesis — what the 2026-07-05 forced-seller
  scan did).
- **Foreign / non-English filings, tiny ADRs** (a language barrier LLMs erase).
- **Multimodal alt-data** (satellite, foot-traffic, product imagery) — frontier +
  capacity-limited.

## Directives for the lab (what to test next, what to stop testing)

1. **Stop proposing crowded-text edges** (news/headline sentiment, naive 10-K
   sentiment). They fail the barrier test — G1/decay.
2. **Favor structural forced-seller hypotheses** (the durable barrier) — these
   also satisfy the house's G2 constraint gate. Core research weight goes here.
3. **Pilot one capability-frontier hypothesis at a time** — full-document
   synthesis or agentic cross-referencing on neglected names — as a *window* bet,
   measured for decay from day one.
4. **Watch the two gaps as radar:** frontier-capability vs deployed-practice (where
   windows open), and LLM-good-at-reading vs bad-at-execution (build only in the
   first, guard the second).
5. **Read A/B decay as the rotate signal.** When an LLM-lift trends toward zero,
   the edge is crowding — the lab should already be testing the next barrier.

The moat is not any god. It's this: *point the search at durable and frontier
barriers, measure ruthlessly, and rotate off decay before the crowd finishes the
job.*
